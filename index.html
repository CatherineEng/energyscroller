Energy is measured in kilowatt hours, or kWh.

1 kWh = 1000 watts used in one hour.
This is like using a hairdryer for 1 whole hour.

Or watching YouTube on your laptop for 24 hours.

Or driving an electric vehicle for 3-4 miles
You can generate energy also.
1 kWh = pedaling fast on a stationary bike for 10 hours
1 kWh = going on a 9 mile run.

1 kWh = climbing stairs to the top of the Empire State Building 3 times!

Another way to think about it... 1 kWh is the energy equivalent of about 860 calories, or the caloric content ofâ€¦

1 large cheeseburger = 1 kWh

2 standard-sized cupcakes = 1 kWh

1 kWh = the energy your brain needs to think for 2 days!
Now let's go a little bigger.

The average U.S. household uses about 30 kWh per day.

If every one of these icons represented the energy usage of one U.S. household... you would need to scroll this page for 8 hours before reaching the total amount of energy used in the U.S. in one day!
So, let's make these worth more... say, 14x more or 417 kWh each.

With 417 kWh, you can power every home on your block for one day

You can bake 1000 pizzas!

You can drive from Los Angeles to the Oregon border, and back!

And, it will only take you 30 minutes to scroll 11.1 billion kWh!
